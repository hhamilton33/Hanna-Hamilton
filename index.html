My data science journey began as I was completing my BS in Industrial Engineering from the University of Wisconsin-Madison. I was enrolled in an introductory data science course and was fascinated as I learned about techniques such as k-means clustering, apriori algorithm, and decision trees. I steered away from the job search process, quickly prepared for the GRE, and applied to masters programs because I just wanted to learn more. I attended Georgia Tech for my MS in Computational Data Analytics. During this time, I had the opportunity to work as a graduate teaching assistant for Regression Analysis, a graduate level course about various regression models and variable selection techniques, as well as their implementations with R. I also joined a research group consisting of ISyE professors and other graduate students to analyze various hybrid instructional mode strategies and inform campus planners during the COVID-19 pandemic. We compared the strategies from both health and academic perspectives, by their effects on various groups of students, and based on the trade-off between health risk and academic burden. In my free time, I enjoy practicing yoga, hiking, and going to beaches. This year, I have been doing a lot of traveling in Hawaii. I love exploring new places, trying new foods, and meeting people from all over the world. Although I do love Minnesota, it is always a good idea for me to take breaks from the long and cold Minnesota winters :)
